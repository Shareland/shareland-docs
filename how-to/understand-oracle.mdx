# Understanding the Oracle

The Shareland oracle is the engine that prices SQFT tokens. It aggregates real estate transaction data to produce a live, reliable price per square foot for each market.

## Data sources

The oracle pulls from multiple data sources including recorded home sales and listing data across US markets. Data is weighted by recency, transaction volume, and geographic relevance.

## Price updates

Oracle prices update on a regular cadence. You'll see price charts reflecting these updates in real time on the exchange.

## Market halts

If the oracle detects an anomaly — such as a sudden price spike that falls outside expected parameters — the market may automatically halt to protect investors. Halts will be displayed in the exchange UI and we may send notifications depending on your account notification settings.

Markets resume once the anomaly is investigated and resolved.

## Oracle vs. Market Price

The **Oracle Price** represents the true underlying value of the real estate per square foot.

*   **Debt Calculation:** Your debt position (collateralization ratio) is always calculated using the Oracle Price, not the secondary market trading price.
*   **Liquidation:** Liquidations are triggered ONLY if the Oracle Price moves significantly enough to drop your ratio below 110%. Exchange market volatility does not trigger liquidation.

## Methodology

Shareland's oracle methodology was developed in collaboration with researchers at the Wharton School. The approach uses academically validated models for neighborhood-level real estate pricing.
