# Understanding the Oracle

The Shareland oracle is the engine that prices SQFT tokens. It aggregates real estate transaction data to produce a live, reliable price per square foot for each market.

  <img src="/images/screenshots/oracle-chart.png" alt="Oracle price chart" />
</Frame>

## Data sources

The oracle pulls from multiple data sources — recorded home sales, active listing data, and pricing signals across US markets. Data is weighted by recency, transaction volume, and geographic relevance to produce a single authoritative $/sqft price per geography.

## Price updates

Oracle prices update on a continuous basis. You'll see price charts reflecting these updates in real time on the exchange. Historical oracle data is available on every market's detail panel.

## Neighborhood breakdown

Each market is composed of sub-neighborhoods, each with their own $/sqft weighting. The market detail panel shows the individual neighborhood data contributing to the overall market price.

## Market halts

If the oracle detects an anomaly — such as a sudden price movement outside expected parameters — trading may automatically halt for that market. You'll receive an email notification if any market you hold positions in halts. Markets resume once the anomaly is investigated and resolved.

<Note>
  Market halts are a safety mechanism. Your tokens remain in your wallet and are unaffected during a halt.
</Note>

## Research foundation

Shareland's oracle methodology was developed in collaboration with researchers at the Wharton School, including Dr. Susan Wachter and Dr. Andrey Pavlov — two of the world's leading real estate economists. The approach uses academically validated models for neighborhood-level residential real estate pricing.
