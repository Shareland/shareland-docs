# Minting, Borrowing, and Burning

On Shareland, "borrowing" and "minting" refer to the same action: creating new SQFT tokens by locking collateral.

## What is Minting?

Minting is the process of creating new SQFT tokens. When you mint, you are effectively borrowing from the protocol.

1.  **Lock Collateral:** You deposit USDC into the protocol vault.
2.  **Mint SQFT:** The protocol issues new SQFT tokens to your wallet.
3.  **Debt Position:** You now have a debt position equal to the value of the minted tokens. To unlock your collateral, you must return (burn) the same number of SQFT tokens.

## Collateralization & Liquidation

To ensure the system remains solvent, all minted positions must be over-collateralized.

*   **Minimum Collateralization Ratio (MCR):** 110%
    *   This is the liquidation threshold. If your collateral value falls below 110% of your debt value, your position may be liquidated.
*   **Recommendation:** We strongly recommend minting at a ratio of **120% or higher** to provide a safety buffer against price movements.

<Warning>
  **Liquidation Risk:** If the price of SQFT rises (increasing your debt value) or the value of your collateral falls, your ratio decreases. Monitor your health factor closely.
</Warning>

## Burning (Closing a Position)

To close your position and retrieve your collateral:

1.  **Burn Tokens:** You send SQFT tokens back to the protocol.
2.  **Unlock Collateral:** The protocol releases your deposited USDC.
3.  **Profit/Loss:** If the SQFT price has dropped since you minted, you can buy back the tokens for less than you sold them for, keeping the difference as profit (shorting).

## Example

*   **Collateral:** You deposit $1,100 USDC.
*   **Mint:** You mint $1,000 worth of SQFT tokens.
*   **Ratio:** Your collateralization ratio is 110% ($1,100 / $1,000).
*   This is the minimum. To be safe, you should deposit more collateral (e.g., $1,200) or mint fewer tokens.
